High Correlations:: Lower Income + No Healthcare | Smoking | Overweight
There is a high correlation between individuals with a lower income, smoking, obesity and a lack of healthcare. Upon further analysis there is an even higher correlation in the southern states with Texas possibly being an outlier.
Grouped Values:: Average Age + No Healthcare | Smoking | Overweight
For this dataset the average age for most states is between 36 and 42. Utah, North Dakota, Arkansas and the District of Columbia's average age is lower than the median 30-36 years. Florida, New Hampshire, Maine, Vermont and West Virginia median age is above 42. Georgia, Louisiana, Mississippi and Texas show the highest percentage of individuals without healthcare. There seems to be a slight positive correlation between the average age, smoking and obesity. Additionally there is a slight negative correlation between the average age and lack of healthcare.
